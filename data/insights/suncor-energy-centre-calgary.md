# Suncor Energy Centre: Calgary's Landmark Dual-Tower Complex

The Suncor Energy Centre is one of Calgary's most recognizable commercial addresses — twin glass towers anchoring the western edge of the downtown core at 555 4th Avenue SW. It's not just an office building; it's a symbol of Calgary's energy sector identity, even as the city's economy diversifies.

If you're evaluating the Suncor Energy Centre for your Calgary office, here's what you need to know.

## Building Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Address** | 555 4th Avenue SW, Calgary, AB |
| **Classification** | Class A |
| **Towers** | Two towers (East and West) |
| **Floors** | 27+ floors per tower |
| **Total SF** | Approximately 1 million+ square feet combined |
| **Year Built** | Mid-1980s (major renovations completed) |
| **Parking** | Underground parking structure; additional nearby |
| **Transit** | +15 connected; CTrain (Centre Street Station) within walking distance |
| **Building Manager** | Brookfield Properties |

The Suncor Energy Centre is a dual-tower complex that has anchored Calgary's downtown core for decades. Both towers feature modernized lobbies and common areas from renovations in the 2010s, keeping the property competitive with newer Class A buildings in the market.

## Location and Access

The Suncor Energy Centre occupies a prime position at the corner of 4th Avenue and 5th Street SW — the western edge of the downtown financial core, transitioning into the West End.

**Key location advantages:**
- **+15 pedestrian network** connects the building to the broader downtown tunnel system, keeping tenants sheltered from Calgary's weather for much of the year
- **CTrain access** — the Centre Street Station and 7th Avenue corridor are within a 5-7 minute walk
- **Vehicle access** — immediate connection to Bow Trail (Highway 1A) and the broader Deerfoot Trail (Highway 2) for suburban commuters
- **Airport** — approximately 25 minutes to Calgary International Airport in normal traffic

**Nearby amenities:**
- **Simmons Building** (adjacent) — home to popular restaurants (Una Pizza, Monarda, Eat Stock)
- **Kensington Road** — 10-minute walk west to Calgary's best restaurant corridor
- **The Core Shopping Centre** — connected via +15 or a 5-minute street-level walk
- **Bow River pathways** — 5-minute walk north to the river and trail system

## Current Market Position

The Suncor Energy Centre maintains a strong position in Calgary's Class A office market. Here's what tenants and market observers say about it:

**Strengths:**
- Premium address with strong institutional identity
- Excellent parking ratio for a downtown core building
- Modernized common areas and mechanical systems from previous renovations
- +15 connectivity makes it attractive for firms with staff commuting by transit
- Large floor plates (20,000+ square feet) accommodate larger tenants

**Market considerations:**
- Competitive with other Class A towers on 4th Avenue corridor (Bankers Hall, Petro-Canada Place)
- Lease rates reflect the premium address — expect to pay for the location
- Larger floor plates mean more competition from major tenants with large space requirements; smaller firms may find the building less suitable

**Typical lease rates (2026):**
- **Class A asking rent:** $38-52 CAD/ft²/year (NNN)
- **Net effective rent:** After concessions (free rent, TI allowance), effective rates may land in the $32-42 range depending on term and tenant credit
- **Operating costs:** Estimated $14-18/ft²/year in addition to base rent (confirm current year with landlord)

> **Important:** These are market indicative ranges as of early 2026. Actual lease rates depend on floor, view, condition, lease term, tenant credit, and market negotiations. Always verify current figures with a qualified broker.

## How It Compares to Nearby Buildings

**vs. Bankers Hall (855 2nd St SW):**
Bankers Hall is more central in the financial core. Suncor Energy Centre has better +15 connectivity to the western tunnel system and easier access to the Bow Trail corridor. Bankers Hall has the advantage of being more central to the core's restaurant and service density.

**vs. Petro-Canada Place (150 6th Ave SW):**
Both are Class A towers on the west side of downtown. Petro-Canada Place is at the edge of the West End with easy access to the Beltline. Suncor Energy Centre has larger floor plates and is more firmly in the core.

**vs. Telus Sky (685 Centre St S):**
Telus Sky is newer (opened 2020s) with a distinctive architectural identity. It's at the western edge of the core near East Village. Suncor Energy Centre has more established infrastructure and is positioned in the established office core rather than the transitional East Village area.

## How to Approach a Lease at the Suncor Energy Centre

**Step 1: Confirm your requirements.**
How much space do you need? What term? Do you need the larger floor plates the building offers?

**Step 2: Get current market data.**
Lease rates and availability change. A qualified broker can pull current numbers from the building's leasing team and compare them against competing options.

Class A leases tend to be longer-term and less flexible. Understand what's negotiable before you sign.
