# Eighth Avenue Place: Calgary's Modern Class A Landmark

Eighth Avenue Place at 525 8th Avenue SW is one of Calgary's newest Class A office towers — a 25-storey glass and steel building directly across from Bankers Hall, delivering modern office space to Calgary's most prestigious office corridor. Built in the 2000s, it's the kind of building companies picture when they think "modern downtown Calgary office."

If you're evaluating Eighth Avenue Place for your Calgary office, here's what you need to know.

## Building Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Address** | 525 8th Avenue SW, Calgary, AB |
| **Classification** | Class A |
| **Towers** | Two towers (East and West Tower) |
| **Floors** | 25+ floors |
| **Total SF** | Approximately 750,000 square feet combined |
| **Year Built** | 2007 (East Tower), 2012 (West Tower) |
| **Parking** | Underground parking; managed by building |
| **Transit** | +15 connected; CTrain (8th Street and 3rd Street W stations) |
| **Building Manager** | Brookfield Properties |

Eighth Avenue Place is the newest major Class A office complex in Calgary's financial core — built in two phases (East Tower 2007, West Tower 2012) with modern systems, quality finishes, and the kind of building management standards you'd expect from a landmark property. It's the primary competitor to Bankers Hall for companies seeking a premium downtown address with modern construction.

## Location and Access

Eighth Avenue Place sits directly on Stephen Avenue Walk — Calgary's most important office corridor and the city's premier address for business. The 8th Avenue SW corridor is one of the most well-connected locations in Calgary.

**Key location advantages:**
- **Stephen Avenue Walk** — literally on Calgary's main office corridor; full-service restaurants, banks, shops, and services literally at street level
- **CTrain** — 8th Street Station immediately adjacent; 3rd Street W Station within a block; the entire CTrain network accessible
- **+15 network** — connects to Calgary's downtown tunnel system from the building's lobby
- **Downtown parking** — underground parking structure managed by the building
- **Bankers Hall adjacency** — directly across 8th Avenue from Calgary's most prestigious address; walking distance to everything in the core

**Nearby amenities:**
- **Stephen Avenue** — the building's address means full access to Calgary's best restaurant and service corridor
- **The Core Shopping Centre** — one block north via Stephen Avenue
- **Bankers Hall podium** — connected via street level, including food court access
- **Olympic Plaza** — 2-minute walk south
- **Arts District** — adjacent to Calgary's cultural venues

## Current Market Position

Eighth Avenue Place competes directly with Bankers Hall for premium Class A tenants who want the Stephen Avenue address but with modern construction and systems. The building's two-tower configuration gives it flexibility that single-tower buildings don't have.

**Strengths:**
- Newest major Class A building on Stephen Avenue (2000s-2012 construction)
- Modern building systems — HVAC, electrical, elevators all updated
- Stephen Avenue address is Calgary's most prestigious
- Excellent CTrain access with multiple stations nearby
- Strong institutional management (Brookfield Properties)
- Two-tower configuration provides options as tenants grow or contract

**Market considerations:**
- Competitive with Bankers Hall — rents are at similar premium levels
- Limited availability at times — premium address means high occupancy
- The newer construction premium may not be worth it for all tenants compared to well-maintained older buildings

**Typical lease rates (2026):**
- **Class A asking rent:** $42-55 CAD/ft²/year (NNN)
- **Net effective rent:** $36-48 CAD/ft²/year after concessions, depending on term and tenant profile
- **Operating costs:** Estimated $14-18/ft²/year in addition to base rent

> **Important:** These are market indicative ranges as of early 2026. Actual lease rates depend on tower, floor, view, condition, lease term, tenant credit, and market negotiations. Always verify current figures with a qualified broker.

Eighth Avenue Place has attracted a similar tenant profile to Bankers Hall — major financial institutions, law firms, professional services firms, and large corporate tenants. The building's newer construction makes it particularly attractive to firms updating their office environments after older leases expire.

## How It Compares to Nearby Buildings

**vs. Bankers Hall (855 2nd St SW):**
Bankers Hall is older (1989 construction) but equally prestigious and more centrally positioned on Stephen Avenue. Eighth Avenue Place has newer construction (2007-2012). Both command similar rental rates. Bankers Hall may offer slightly more central positioning; Eighth Avenue Place offers modern systems and slightly newer building aesthetics.

**vs. Bow Valley Square (205 5th Ave SW):**
Bow Valley Square is significantly older (1970s-1980s construction) and positioned slightly east of the most central core. Eighth Avenue Place offers newer construction, Stephen Avenue address, and more modern systems at a higher rate. Bow Valley Square offers better value; Eighth Avenue Place offers more modern space.

**vs. Suncor Energy Centre (555 4th Ave SW):**
Both are twin-tower Class A complexes. Suncor Energy Centre is at the western edge of the core; Eighth Avenue Place is at the most central point of Stephen Avenue. Suncor has larger floor plates; Eighth Avenue Place has newer construction and more central positioning.

## How to Approach a Lease at Eighth Avenue Place

**Step 1: Confirm your budget.**
Eighth Avenue Place is premium-priced. Make sure your budget is realistic for the Stephen Avenue address before you start the process.

**Step 2: Compare to Bankers Hall.**
Before committing to Eighth Avenue Place, see what Bankers Hall offers. The two buildings compete directly for the same tenants, and sometimes one has better availability or better terms at a given moment.

**Step 3: Evaluate the two-tower options.**
With two towers, you may have options across both. The East and West towers may have different availabilities and economics.

**Step 4: Consider total cost, not just base rent.**
Premium base rent with meaningful concessions may be better economics than lower base rent with fewer incentives.
