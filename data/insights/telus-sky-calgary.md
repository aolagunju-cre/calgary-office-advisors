# Telus Sky: Calgary's Architectural Landmark at the Edge of Downtown

Telus Sky at 685 Centre Street S is not just an office building — it's a statement. Designed by the internationally renowned Bjarke Ingels Group (BIG), the tower's sculpted form redefined Calgary's western skyline when it opened in the early 2020s. The building mixes residential and office components in a way that Calgary hadn't seen before, creating a genuine mixed-use landmark at the point where downtown transitions into East Village.

If you're evaluating Telus Sky for your Calgary office, here's what you need to know.

## Building Overview

| Attribute | Detail |

| **Classification** | Class A (mixed-use tower) |
| **Towers** | Single tower (residential + office) |
| **Floors** | 59 floors total; office floors in lower and mid sections |
| **Total SF** | Approximately 370,000 ft² (office component ~100,000 ft² estimated) |
| **Year Built** | 2022 (opened early 2020s) |

Telus Sky is Calgary's newest and most architecturally distinctive office building. The tower's signature curved form and the Bjarke Ingels Group name bring a level of design prestige that Calgary's office market hasn't had before. The office component occupies the lower and mid sections of the tower; the upper floors are residential. This mixed-use configuration creates an active environment that single-use office towers don't have.

## Location and Access

Telus Sky sits at the corner of Centre Street S and 7th Avenue S — at the western edge of the downtown core, adjacent to East Village, and within walking distance of the Bow River and the New Central Library.

**Key location advantages:**
- **East Village adjacency** — the building sits at the edge of East Village, walking distance to the New Central Library, Studio Bell, and the area's growing restaurant and retail scene
- **CTrain** — City Hall Station is a 3-minute walk; the 7th Avenue line connects to the entire CTrain network
- **Bow River pathways** — a 5-minute walk north to Calgary's best recreational asset
- **Downtown core** — a 10-minute walk west brings you to Stephen Avenue and the heart of the financial district
- **Vehicle access** — immediate connection to Centre Street S and the broader downtown road network

**Nearby amenities:**
- **East Village** — National Music Centre, New Central Library, restaurants and cafes in the library district
- **Kensington Road** — 10-minute walk west along 7th Avenue S
- **Beltline** — 15-minute walk south to 17th Avenue SW
- **River pathways** — immediate access to the Bow River pathway system

## Current Market Position

Telus Sky occupies a unique niche in Calgary's Class A market — it's the newest building, has the most distinctive architecture, and is positioned at the intersection of downtown and East Village. The building attracts tenants who want something different from the traditional glass tower in the financial district.

**Strengths:**
- Calgary's newest office building with architecturally distinctive design
- Bjarke Ingels Group association brings international design prestige
- East Village positioning offers urban energy and growing amenity base
- Modern building systems throughout
- Mixed-use environment (residential above office) creates active, interesting base

**Market considerations:**
- Office inventory is limited — smaller floor plates than major towers
- Premium pricing reflecting the newer construction and architectural prestige
- East Village office market is still developing — fewer comparable tenants
- Building is more isolated from the dense restaurant and service concentration of Stephen Avenue

**Typical lease rates (2026):**
- **Class A asking rent:** $42-55 CAD/ft²/year (NNN)
- **Net effective rent:** $36-48 CAD/ft²/year after concessions, depending on term and tenant profile
- **Operating costs:** Estimated $14-18/ft²/year in addition to base rent

> **Important:** These are market indicative ranges as of early 2026. Actual lease rates depend on floor, view, condition, lease term, tenant credit, and market negotiations. Always verify current figures with a qualified broker.

## How It Compares to Nearby Buildings

**vs. Eighth Avenue Place (525 8th Ave SW):**
Both are modern Class A towers, but the comparison is close in some respects and different in others. Eighth Avenue Place is on Stephen Avenue (more central, denser service concentration). Telus Sky has better architecture (Bjarke Ingels Group vs. conventional architecture) but is less central and more isolated. Eighth Avenue Place has more traditional office floor plates; Telus Sky has more distinctive character. The pricing is similar.

**vs. Suncor Energy Centre (555 4th Ave SW):**
Suncor Energy Centre is larger, more established, and positioned in the core. Telus Sky has the design advantage (Bjarke Ingels Group vs. conventional architecture) and newer systems. Suncor has larger floor plates; Telus Sky has more distinctive character. The pricing is similar.

**vs. East Village buildings:**
Telus Sky is the most prominent building in the East Village-adjacent area, and it's newer and more architecturally distinguished than anything else nearby. If you want an East Village or near-East Village address with the highest quality building, this is it.

## How to Approach a Lease at Telus Sky

**Step 1: Confirm your size requirements.**
Telus Sky's office floor plates are more modest than major Class A towers. Make sure the building can accommodate your space before you invest time in the process.

**Step 2: Understand the East Village positioning.**
This is not a Stephen Avenue building. If you need to be in the financial district core, look at Eighth Avenue Place or Bankers Hall instead. If you want East Village adjacency with better architecture, Telus Sky fits that brief.

**Step 3: Evaluate the design value.**
For companies that want to use their office environment as a recruiting and retention tool, Telus Sky's architectural quality may justify a premium.

**Step 4: Compare total cost to alternatives.**
Telus Sky commands a premium. Make sure the premium is justified for your specific business needs versus Eighth Avenue Place or other Class A options.
