# Petro-Canada Place: Calgary's West-End Class A Tower

Petro-Canada Place stands at 150 6th Avenue SW — a Class A office tower positioned at the western edge of Calgary's downtown core, where the office district transitions into the Beltline's urban density. It's one of the buildings that bridges two of Calgary's most active commercial environments: the downtown office core and the Beltline's restaurant, retail, and residential energy.

If you're evaluating Petro-Canada Place for your Calgary office, here's what you need to know.

## Building Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Address** | 150 6th Avenue SW, Calgary, AB |
| **Classification** | Class A |
| **Towers** | Single tower |
| **Floors** | 26 floors |
| **Total SF** | Approximately 430,000 square feet |
| **Year Built** | 1984 (major renovations completed) |
| **Parking** | Underground parking; above-grade parking adjacent |
| **Transit** | +15 connected; CTrain (8th Street Station) nearby |
| **Building Manager** | Brookfield Properties |

Petro-Canada Place is one of Calgary's older Class A towers — built in the mid-1980s but regularly updated through renovations to lobby, common areas, and building systems. It's a well-managed, well-maintained building that competes effectively with newer towers through consistent property management and competitive pricing.

## Location and Access

Petro-Canada Place occupies a distinctive position at the corner of 6th Avenue and 1st Street SW — the transition zone between the downtown core and the Beltline, and a short walk from the Bow River pathway system.

**Key location advantages:**
- **+15 network** connects the building to downtown's tunnel system, with connections westward into the Beltline
- **CTrain** — 8th Street Station on the 8th Avenue corridor is a 3-minute walk; the 7th Avenue line connects to the broader transit network
- **Beltline adjacency** — a 5-minute walk takes you to the 17th Avenue SW corridor and its restaurants, services, and urban energy
- **Bow River pathways** — one of Calgary's best recreational assets is a 7-minute walk north
- **Vehicle access** — immediate connection to 6th Avenue one-way westbound; Bow Trail and the broader west Calgary road network

**Nearby amenities:**
- **Beltline restaurants** — 17th Avenue SW and the 4th Street corridor are both within 5-10 minutes walking
- **Kensington Road** — 10-minute walk west
- **Simmons Building** — 8-minute walk south (Una Pizza, Monarda, Eat Stock)
- **Downtown retail** — connected to the Core via +15 or street-level walk

## Current Market Position

Petro-Canada Place occupies a competitive but distinctive niche in Calgary's Class A market — it offers the quality of a downtown Class A building with better value positioning than the most central towers, plus proximity to the Beltline's urban amenities.

**Strengths:**
- Class A quality at more accessible pricing than central core towers
- Beltline adjacency gives tenants access to the best restaurant and service density in Calgary
- Above-grade and underground parking options
- Brookfield Properties management provides consistent, professional building standards
- Good transit access without being in the most congested part of downtown
- Above-grade parking is unusual in the downtown core — a genuine convenience for car-commuting staff

**Market considerations:**
- At the western edge of the core — some clients and staff may perceive it as less central
- Single tower configuration (no campus effect like Bow Valley Square or Suncor)
- Base building age shows in some elements despite renovations
- Competitive with Beltline Class B buildings for smaller tenants who want better positioning

**Typical lease rates (2026):**
- **Class A asking rent:** $32-44 CAD/ft²/year (NNN)
- **Net effective rent:** $27-38 CAD/ft²/year after concessions, depending on term and tenant profile
- **Operating costs:** Estimated $12-15/ft²/year in addition to base rent

> **Important:** These are market indicative ranges as of early 2026. Actual lease rates depend on floor, view, condition, lease term, tenant credit, and market negotiations. Always verify current figures with a qualified broker.

## How It Compares to Nearby Buildings

**vs. Suncor Energy Centre (555 4th Ave SW):**
Both are Class A west-side towers. Suncor Energy Centre is more prominent (twin towers, larger floor plates) and positioned more firmly in the core. Petro-Canada Place is slightly less central but offers better Beltline amenity access and above-grade parking. Suncor Energy Centre commands higher rent; Petro-Canada Place offers better value.

**vs. Eighth Avenue Place (525 8th Ave SW):**
Eighth Avenue Place is a newer building (2000s construction) directly across from Bankers Hall — more central and more prestigious. Petro-Canada Place offers better value with slightly less central positioning. Both have parking; Petro-Canada Place's above-grade option is unique.

**vs. Beltline Class B buildings:**
For tenants considering Class B space in the Beltline, Petro-Canada Place offers a meaningful quality step-up at a manageable rent premium. If you're weighing Beltline Class B versus Petro-Canada Place Class A, the building quality and parking advantages often justify the difference.
