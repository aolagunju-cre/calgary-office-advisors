# Bankers Hall: Calgary's Financial Core Anchor

Bankers Hall is Calgary's most recognized financial district address — twin towers at 855 2nd Street SW that have anchored the city's business core since the late 1980s. When people picture Calgary's office skyline, Bankers Hall is often the building they see in their mind.

For businesses evaluating Bankers Hall as a potential office home, here's the complete picture.

## Building Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Address** | 855 2nd Street SW, Calgary, AB |
| **Classification** | Class A |
| **Towers** | Two towers (East and West) |
| **Floors** | 30+ floors per tower |
| **Total SF** | Approximately 1.5 million square feet combined |
| **Year Built** | 1989 (regularly updated common areas) |
| **Parking** | Underground parking; managed by building |
| **Transit** | +15 connected; CTrain (3rd Street W Station) immediate access |
| **Building Manager** | Brookfield Properties |

Bankers Hall is one of Calgary's largest Class A office complexes, positioned in the absolute heart of the downtown financial district. The towers are connected via a common podium with retail, services, and conference facilities. The building has undergone regular renovations to common areas and mechanical systems, keeping it competitive with newer towers in the market.

## Location and Access

Bankers Hall occupies the most central position in Calgary's office core — 2nd Street SW between 8th and 9th Avenue, directly adjacent to the 8th Avenue transit corridor and steps from the +15 pedestrian network.

**Key location advantages:**
- **Immediate +15 access** — the building connects directly to Calgary's downtown tunnel system, providing sheltered access to the entire downtown core, the food court network, and connection points to the East Village
- **CTrain** — 3rd Street W Station is directly adjacent; 7th Avenue line is a 2-minute walk; Stephen Avenue stations are 5 minutes away
- **Stephen Avenue Walk** — the building opens onto Stephen Avenue, Calgary's premier office corridor and the city's most vibrant restaurant and service concentration
- **Vehicle access** — immediate access to the 8th Avenue one-way corridor connecting to Bow Trail; downtown parking structure access nearby

**Nearby amenities:**
- **Stephen Avenue** — full-service restaurants, coffee shops, banks, services literally at your doorstep
- **The Core Shopping Centre** — one block north via Stephen Avenue
- **Olympic Plaza** — one block south for public events and green space
- **Arts District** — proximity to the Calgary Arts District, Theatre Centre, and related cultural venues

## Current Market Position

Bankers Hall is considered a tier-1 Calgary Class A building — one of the buildings companies target when they want a genuine downtown core address with maximum prestige and transit convenience.

**Strengths:**
- The most central Class A position in Calgary's office market
- Stephen Avenue address is synonymous with Calgary's business identity
- Excellent CTrain access with multiple stations within walking distance
- Strong institutional management (Brookfield Properties)
- Diverse tenant mix spanning financial services, professional services, energy, and tech

**Market considerations:**
- Premium pricing — you're paying for the best address in Calgary
- Heavy traffic in and around Stephen Avenue during peak hours
- Parking is expensive and competitive
- Lower floors in the East Tower face Stephen Avenue — can be noisy

**Typical lease rates (2026):**
- **Class A asking rent:** $40-55 CAD/ft²/year (NNN)
- **Net effective rent:** $35-48 CAD/ft²/year after concessions, depending on term and tenant profile
- **Operating costs:** Estimated $14-18/ft²/year in addition to base rent

> **Important:** These are market indicative ranges as of early 2026. Actual lease rates depend on floor, view, condition, lease term, tenant credit, and market negotiations. Always verify current figures with a qualified broker.

## Tenant Mix

Bankers Hall historically has one of the most prestigious and diverse tenant lists in Calgary — ranging from major Canadian banks and institutional investment firms to national law firms, major energy companies, and increasingly tech and innovation-sector tenants. The building's vacancy tends to be lower than market average, reflecting strong demand for the address.

## How It Compares to Nearby Buildings

**vs. Suncor Energy Centre (555 4th Ave SW):**
Suncor Energy Centre is at the western edge of the core, more focused on the Bow Trail commuter corridor. Bankers Hall is in the densest part of the financial district with Stephen Avenue access. Suncor has larger floor plates; Bankers Hall has better transit positioning and a more central address.

**vs. Bow Valley Square (205 5th Ave SW):**
Bow Valley Square is just east of Bankers Hall on 5th Avenue — slightly less central but still in the core. Both are Brookfield Properties managed buildings. Bow Valley Square has a more campus-like feel with its four-tower complex.

**vs. Eighth Avenue Place (525 8th Ave SW):**
Eighth Avenue Place is directly across the street from Bankers Hall — a newer Class A tower that competes directly for the same tenant profile. Both are premium addresses; Eighth Avenue Place has newer construction (2000s) while Bankers Hall has the established prestige.

## How to Approach a Lease at Bankers Hall

**Step 1: Understand the premium.**
Bankers Hall commands the highest rents in Calgary. Make sure your budget is realistic for this address before you start touring.

**Step 2: Know your space requirements.**
The building has varying floor plate sizes. Understanding whether you need a full floor or a multi-tenant suite helps you evaluate options within the building.

**Step 3: Compare net effective costs.**
A lower base rent with fewer concessions may cost you more over the lease term than a higher base rent with meaningful TI and free rent. Calculate the total cost, not just the sticker rate.

**Step 4: Consider the competition.**
Before committing to Bankers Hall, see what Eighth Avenue Place, Bow Valley Square, and other nearby Class A buildings offer. Sometimes the same tenant profile can get better terms in adjacent buildings.
