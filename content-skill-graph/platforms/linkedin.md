# LinkedIn — Calgary Office Advisors

## Platform DNA
- No hard character limit. sweet spot: 1,300-2,000 chars for posts
- Vibe: professional but human. opinions and takes win here. not a content feed, a conversation
- Audience: Calgary business owners, operations leaders, CFOs, CRE professionals

## Content Rules
- Lead with a specific take or observation. "The Calgary market is X" not "Here are some thoughts on..."
- First line is EVERYTHING. LinkedIn truncates after ~210 chars. hook must be in first line
- Use [[hooks]] — market data hooks and negotiation hooks perform best
- Match [[brand-voice]] but more opinionated. LinkedIn rewards clear takes
- Short paragraphs. one idea per paragraph.
- No hashtags in body. max 3 at end.
- Links in first comment, not in the post.

## Formats That Work

1. **Market Take** — clear opinion on current Calgary CRE conditions + why it matters for tenants
2. **Negotiation Tip** — specific advice on what to negotiate in a Calgary lease
3. **Tenant Mistake** — "most tenants get X wrong" + what to do instead
4. **Case Study** — anonymized real deal, what happened, what was learned
5. **Neighborhood Comparison** — specific comparison between Calgary areas

## Posting Strategy
- Frequency: 3-4x/week
- Best times: 7-8am, 12pm, 5-6pm MT
- Comment on 5-10 relevant posts before publishing yours

## Audience on LinkedIn
- Primary: [[tenants]] — business owners, operations, CFOs making real estate decisions
- Secondary: [[cre-professionals]] — brokers, agents following the market
- They want: accurate Calgary CRE info, negotiation tactics, market intelligence
- They don't want: generic CRE content, landlord marketing, empty optimism

## Repurposing Notes
- LinkedIn is the SECOND step in the [[repurpose]] chain
- Take the blog article's key insight → find the TAKE → write a narrative post
- Don't paste the article. LinkedIn readers want engagement, not just information
