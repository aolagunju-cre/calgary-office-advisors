# Blog — Calgary Office Advisors

## Platform DNA
- SEO-optimized long-form articles. sweet spot: 2,000-3,000 words
- Vibe: educational, comprehensive, data-driven. the definitive answer to the search query
- Audience: [[tenants]] searching for information about Calgary office space. tenants in research mode

## Content Rules
- SEO research first. primary keyword in: title, first 100 words, 2-3 H2s, meta description
- Keyword density: 1-2% naturally integrated
- Structure: clear H1 > H2 > H3 hierarchy, subheadings every 300-400 words
- Data: cite sources, use real numbers where possible, caveat estimates clearly
- Internal links: 3-5 links to other COA articles or Calgary resources
- External links: 2-3 authority links to credible CRE sources

## Article Structure

```
[H1: Primary keyword, clear topic]

[Intro: 100-150 words — hook, why this matters for Calgary tenants, what they'll learn]

[H2: First major section]
  [Content with natural keyword integration]
  [Bullet lists where appropriate]

[H2: Second major section]
  [Content with data/examples]

[H2: Third major section]
  [Actionable advice or comparison]

[Conclusion: Recap key takeaways, specific CTA — contact, subscribe, etc.]
```

## Meta Elements

- **Meta title:** 50-60 characters, primary keyword
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters, includes keyword + value proposition
- **URL slug:** `/[primary-keyword]-calgary-guide` or similar

## Posting Strategy
- Frequency: 2x/week (Tuesday and Thursday)
- Consistency matters more than frequency for SEO
- Update articles when market conditions change significantly

## Repurposing Notes
- Blog is the ANCHOR piece — the foundation everything else builds on
- LinkedIn post extracts the key take (rewrite hook entirely)
- Newsletter goes deeper with exclusive context not in the article
