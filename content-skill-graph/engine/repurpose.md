# Repurposing Chain — Calgary Office Advisors

One idea enters. Three platform-native pieces come out. Each one THINKS about the topic differently — not reformatting.

## The Chain Order

### Step 1: [[blog]] — Write First
SEO article with keyword research. Structure for search intent, 2,000+ words, data-backed, actionable. This is the foundation piece.
- Output: long-form article (2,000-3,000 words) with H1/H2/H3 structure, internal/external links, meta description
- Focus: keyword integration, search intent, comprehensive coverage of the topic

### Step 2: [[linkedin]] — Adapt the Angle
Take the article's key insight and turn it into a narrative LinkedIn post. Don't paste the article — find the TAKE and lead with that.
- Output: long-form post (1,300-2,000 chars)
- Lead with: a specific deal, a market observation, a clear opinion
- Add: personal perspective the article didn't include

### Step 3: [[newsletter]] — Go Deeper
Take the article, add exclusive behind-the-scenes context. What didn't make it into the article? What's the real story that doesn't fit the SEO structure?
- Output: 1,000-1,500 word email
- Focus: exclusive insights, negotiation tactics, market timing advice, behind-the-scenes

## The Litmus Test

"If someone read the blog post, followed me on LinkedIn, AND received the newsletter, would they feel like they were reading the same thing three times?"

If yes → you're reformatting, not rethinking. The LinkedIn post needs a different angle, the newsletter needs exclusive depth.

## What Actually Changes Between Platforms

| Element | Blog | LinkedIn | Newsletter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Format | Long-form SEO article | Narrative post | Email |
| Length | 2,000-3,000 words | 1,300-2,000 chars | 1,000-1,500 words |
| Angle | Keyword-driven, comprehensive | Opinion/take-driven, engaging | Exclusive, behind-the-scenes |
| Tone | Educational, data-focused | Professional, conversational | Most personal, insider |
| CTA | Depends on article goal | Engage in comments | Reply to email |

## Batch Workflow

1. Run SEO research for the topic
2. Write the blog article first (this is the anchor piece)
3. Extract the key take for LinkedIn — rewrite the hook entirely
4. Add exclusive context for the newsletter (what didn't make it into the article?)
5. Review all three before publishing any
