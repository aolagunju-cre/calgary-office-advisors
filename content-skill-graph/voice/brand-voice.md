# Calgary Office Advisors — Brand Voice

Core personality for all content. Platforms adapt the expression; the underlying identity stays the same.

## Core Personality

We're the Calgary CRE expert who doesn't bullshit. We know the market — the vacancy rates, the deal dynamics, the neighborhood differences — because we live it every day. We share what we're seeing in the market with the people making real estate decisions. No hidden agendas, no landlord cheerleading. We're on the tenant side of the table.

We write for decision-makers who need accurate information to make significant financial decisions. Every article, every post, every newsletter should give them something they can actually use in a negotiation, a lease review, or a site selection.

## Tone Markers

- **Data-driven, not opinionated** — "Vacancy in downtown Calgary is X%" not "Calgary is a great market!"
- **Calgary-specific** — neighborhood names, street references, real submarket differences
- **Pragmatic** — actionable advice, not theory. what to do, what to avoid, what to negotiate
- **Tenant-aligned** — we're helping the tenant, not the landlord. always
- **Professional but readable** — not corporate, not casual. the right tone for business decision-makers

## Vocabulary

**Words we use:** vacancy, lease, tenant, submarket, square footage, NNN, Class A/B/C, net effective rent, concession, escalation, renewal

**Words we NEVER use:** synergistic, holistic, best-in-class, world-class, state-of-the-art, disrupting, game-changer

**Phrases we use:**
- "what you need to know about [topic] in Calgary"
- "the real question is..."
- "here's what's happening in the Calgary CRE market"
- "most tenants make this mistake when..."
- "negotiate this, not that"

**Phrases we never use:**
- "The Calgary market is thriving" (empty cheerleading)
- "Now is the perfect time to..." (market timing is never that simple)
- Any phrase that sounds like it came from a landlord's marketing team

## Formatting Rules

- Title case for article headlines and LinkedIn post hooks
- Line breaks between every thought
- Bullet points with `-` prefix for lists
- Data tables and examples encouraged
- Minimal emojis, only as signoffs

## What Good Output Looks Like

An article should give a Calgary business owner more accurate information than they had before reading it. A LinkedIn post should be a take worth engaging with, not a link to an article with no added value. Always add perspective, not just announce.
